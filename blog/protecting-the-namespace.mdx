Free and open doesn't mean unprotected. A public namespace only works if people can **trust it**. Here's how we keep Stackryze Domains a safe place to build.

## Automated abuse detection

Our systems continuously monitor for:

- **Phishing** attempts targeting users and brands
- **Malware** distribution and command-and-control activity
- **Spam** and bulk-registration patterns
- **Other malicious activity** that violates our usage policy

When something suspicious shows up, our detection systems flag it for review — fast.

## A dedicated Abuse & Safety team

Automation catches the noise. Humans handle the nuance. Our Abuse & Safety team investigates reports, takes action on confirmed violations, and permanently removes repeat offenders from the platform.

## Transparency you can verify

We don't make decisions in the dark.

- **Documented policies** guide every enforcement action
- **A clear reporting process** for anyone to flag abuse
- **Fair appeals** for legitimate users who believe a decision was wrong

If you ever need to report something, write to us at `reportabuse@stackryze.com`.

## Privacy through resilience

We minimize dependencies and prioritize user trust at every layer:

- Self-hosted, geographically distributed systems
- Designed for security, reliability, and long-term resilience
- No third-party data brokers — your information stays where it belongs

## An open internet, with guardrails

We believe everyone deserves access to a trustworthy online identity. By lowering barriers to entry **while** maintaining strong security standards, we help make the internet more open, accessible, and empowering — for everyone.
